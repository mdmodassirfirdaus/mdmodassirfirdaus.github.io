About

Continuum and Soft Roboticis

I am a PhD scholar in Mechanical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar (IITGN). My research centers on the kinematics, dynamics, and control of tendon-driven continuum robots, flexible, bio-inspired manipulators that excel at navigating confined, complex, and curvilinear environments with high precision. I focus on advancing their mechanical design, modeling, and control strategies to enable reliable performance in medical interventions, such as minimally invasive surgery and safe navigation through challenging anatomical pathways.

In my work, I develop innovative methods like virtual actuation space frameworks and tendon mapping strategies to improve inverse kinematics, achieve decoupled section control, enhance trajectory tracking, and ensure robust performance in multi-section continuum systems. My contributions include publications in journals such as Robotica and Journal of Mechanisms and Robotics, along with presentations at conferences like Advances in Robotics, IEEE ROBIO, and an upcoming acceptance at ICRA 2026. I was also recognized with a GYTI TECHPEDIA 2024 Appreciation award for a 3D-printable tendon-driven flexible surgical robot that successfully demonstrated cadaveric trials.

Looking ahead, I am passionate about expanding my expertise across the full spectrum of robotics, including soft robotics, rigid-linked systems, and hybrid designs that combine compliance with structural strength. While I remain deeply committed to advancing robotic-assisted medical procedures through innovations in force feedback, haptic control, AI integration, and intelligent autonomy to improve precision, safety, and patient outcomes. I am equally excited to explore continuum and soft robots in broader domains. These include industrial inspection and maintenance, search-and-rescue operations in unstructured environments, delicate manipulation in manufacturing and agriculture, human-robot collaboration, environmental exploration, and other emerging solutions where adaptability, safe interaction, and morphological computation provide unique advantages over traditional rigid systems.

With a strong foundation in continuum mechanisms and a drive for translational, interdisciplinary impact, I am committed to bridging fundamental research with real-world challenges across healthcare and beyond. I thrive on collaboration and am eager to connect with others passionate about innovative robotics, whether in medical applications or novel frontiers in soft and hybrid systems. Let's shape the future of adaptable, intelligent robotics together!
